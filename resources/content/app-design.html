§ 04 · Google Business Profile Integration spec

Every profile, pre-filled.

Merchants don't type their address, phone, hours or photos. Google Business Profile data flows in via Places API, pre-fills everything, and refreshes automatically. The business adds the one thing Google doesn't have: an offer.

What we pull

The full record.

  • Name displayName
  • Address formattedAddress
  • Phone internationalPhoneNumber
  • Website websiteUri
  • Hours regularOpeningHours
  • Star rating rating
  • Review count userRatingCount
  • Photos photos[] · up to 10
  • Reviews reviews[] · up to 5
Endpoints

Two APIs, simple.

  • Text Search POST /places:searchText
  • Place Details GET /places/{id}
  • Photo GET /places/{id}/photos/{ref}
  • Geocoding GET /geocode/json
  • Maps SDK iOS Free unlimited

Field-mask aggressively. Cache 30 days. Refresh ratings weekly, photos monthly, base fields quarterly. Est. cost £40-80/mo at 500 businesses.

Data flow

Sign up to live profile - under a minute.

The merchant onboarding is a single search field. They type the business name, we find it on Google, pull the full record, they confirm. That's it.

§ 05 · Business Plan One-page summary

The thesis, tight.

A condensed version of the full plan. Free-to-use iOS app helping consumers discover offers from independent local businesses and helping those businesses reach new customers with verified, consented contact data.

Thesis

A free, frictionless marketplace for local indie offers.

Two-sided, both free to start. Consumers find live discounts from indies on a map. Businesses get a self-serve profile pre-filled from Google, a window-sticker QR, and customer email capture on every redemption. Monetises via freemium business tiers, sponsored emails, and AdMob - activated from Month 6 once density proves.

Problem
Indies have no working marketing channel.

Google Maps shows businesses but not their current deals. Facebook Ads need expertise indies don't have. Most local businesses rely on word-of-mouth and have no functional digital acquisition channel.

Solution
Free profile + QR-scan customer capture.

Self-serve onboarding pre-filled from Google. Publish offers in minutes. Every QR scan = a new customer with verified email and consented profile data, flowing to the business portal.

Market
~500K addressable UK indies.

1.5-2M micro indie businesses across retail, hospitality, services, trades. Year 1 reachable in 3 seed towns: ~2,000-3,000 indies, ~400K consumers in Wokingham, Reading + 1.

Model
Free → Freemium → Diversified.
  • Mo. 1-6: Free both sides. Prove density.
  • Mo. 6-12: Business freemium £15-25/mo + sponsored emails.
  • Year 2+: Tiers, white-label councils, affiliate revenue.
Go-to-market
Density first, geography second.

Launch UK-wide in App Store. Actively recruit 50-100 businesses in 2 seed towns. Free channels: BIDs, Chambers, indie associations, local Facebook groups, subreddits, local press. Paid only after density proves the unit economics.

Tech
AI-assisted, self-built, bootstrapped.

SwiftUI or FlutterFlow for iOS. Next.js + Supabase for the portal. Google Maps + Places API for everything geographic. Built with Claude Code. No agency, no employees Year 1.

Costs
Year 1 ≈ £2K-4.8K.
  • Apple Dev £80/yr
  • Hosting + DB £0-300/yr
  • Google Maps API £100-1,000/yr
  • Marketing + brand £500-1,500/yr
  • Legal + admin £200/yr
Top risks
Three things that could kill this.
  • Cold start. Sparse density on both sides. Fix: seed towns first.
  • iOS only. Misses ~50% UK Android share. Fix: Android Mo. 4-6.
  • Founder bandwidth. Side-project pace. Fix: be honest about timeline.
§ 06 · Open Questions To resolve before MVP

What we haven't decided yet.

Six categories of open questions worth thinking through. None block starting - but each will need an answer before public launch. Grouped by the function that needs to weigh in.

Brand & Name
04
  1. Which of the ten logo concepts is the right direction? Is "locolie" the final name, or should we test "Highstreet", "Vicinity", "Postcode Perks" etc.?
  2. Trademark check across the UK IPO and EUIPO for the chosen name and mark.
  3. What's the brand voice - friendly local-business cheerleader, slick marketplace, or something else?
  4. Do we need an explicit "for indies, not chains" position, or does that read as small-minded?
Product
06
  1. Redemption code length and security. 6-digit, 8-digit, signed JWT? What's the spoof risk?
  2. What happens when a business refuses to honour a valid offer? Customer reporting flow + auto-flagging.
  3. How does a customer find which businesses do which categories if Google's data is wrong? Manual override?
  4. Should the app have an AI assistant from day one, or is that a Phase 2 nice-to-have?
  5. Should consumers be able to follow specific businesses for offer notifications?
  6. How do we handle businesses that lose their Google Business Profile listing?
Business Model
05
  1. When does business freemium activate? At launch, Month 3, or Month 6?
  2. What's the right price point for the paid tier - £15, £25, £40/mo?
  3. Do we charge per redemption / capture instead of flat monthly?
  4. What's the price for a sponsored email push to the consumer base, and what's the floor list size?
  5. Should we offer free local-council partnerships in exchange for marketing reach (à la Local Pocket)?
Tech & Build
05
  1. SwiftUI native vs FlutterFlow vs PWA - decide after week-one prototype.
  2. Where does customer data sit, and how does it flow to the business portal? Direct DB or CRM-style export?
  3. What email tooling? Resend? Or integrate with Klaviyo / Mailchimp for sponsored sends?
  4. Push notification rules - when do we send, what frequency cap?
  5. How does the business verify the redemption code at the till - typed in, scanned by the merchant's phone, or auto-redeemed?
Go-to-Market
05
  1. Which two seed towns? Wokingham + Reading is the obvious pair - but is a more affluent third town (Henley) or a denser one (Brighton, Bristol) the right next bet?
  2. How do we get the first 50 businesses signed up? Door-to-door, BID partnership, paid ads to merchants?
  3. What's the launch story for press - "free marketing for indies", "support your high street", or something else?
  4. Do we run paid Meta ads to consumers from day one or wait for density?
  5. Physical QR window stickers - print run size, design, distribution. Who fulfils?
Legal & Ops
06
  1. UK Companies House registration - when, and as Ltd or sole trader to start?
  2. T&Cs and Privacy Policy templates - Rocket Lawyer, Genie AI, or bespoke?
  3. GDPR & consent flows for customer data capture on QR scan. Explicit opt-in language.
  4. VAT registration - only at £90K threshold, or proactively?
  5. Insurance - public liability, professional indemnity, both?
  6. Who owns the data - locolie, the business, or shared? Legal basis for sharing customer emails with merchants?
